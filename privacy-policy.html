2 Information We Do Not Collect

PrivyScan performs all analysis locally on your device. No scan results, app lists, permission data, device identifiers, usage statistics, or any other information is ever sent to any server — including ours.

Specifically, PrivyScan never collects:

  • Your name, email address, or any personally identifiable information
  • The list of apps installed on your device
  • Scan results or risk scores
  • Camera, microphone, or location data
  • Device identifiers (IMEI, Android ID, advertising ID)
  • Usage patterns or analytics events
  • Crash reports or diagnostic data

3 Permissions Explained

PrivyScan requests several Android permissions to perform its analysis. Every permission is used exclusively for on-device scanning. Here is a plain-language explanation of each:

♿
Accessibility Service (PrivyScan Monitor)
Sensitive
What it does: Detects which app is currently in the foreground, and observes when apps access sensitive system services (camera, microphone, location) while running in the background. This powers the real-time Dynamic Monitor feature.

Why it is needed: Android does not provide a public API to detect background sensor access without an Accessibility Service. This is the only mechanism that can monitor real-time privacy behaviour across all apps.
PrivyScan Monitor explicitly does NOT: read screen content, capture screenshots, record keystrokes or passwords, record audio or video, inject gestures, or transmit any observed data off your device. These capabilities are disabled and declared as false in our accessibility service configuration.
📦
QUERY_ALL_PACKAGES
Special
What it does: Allows PrivyScan to see the complete list of installed apps on your device.

Why it is needed: Without this permission, Android 11+ restricts which apps can be listed. PrivyScan needs to see all installed apps to perform a full device-wide privacy scan.
This list never leaves your device. It is read, analysed for permissions and trackers, scored, and displayed — all locally.
📊
PACKAGE_USAGE_STATS
Special
What it does: Allows PrivyScan to read which services are running in the background using UsageStatsManager.

Why it is needed: Used to detect apps that have active background services — a key indicator in the dynamic risk scoring model.
Only service presence is read — not app content, usage duration, or any data produced by those apps.
🔍
GET_APP_OPS_STATS
Special
What it does: Reads AppOps records to detect camera, microphone, and location access events per app.

Why it is needed: This is the primary signal for identifying apps that access sensitive hardware in the background — a core part of hybrid risk scoring.
Only the fact that access occurred is used — not the content captured by the camera, microphone, or location data itself.
🔔
POST_NOTIFICATIONS
Standard
What it does: Allows PrivyScan to display a persistent notification while the monitoring service is active, and to alert you when a high-risk background event is detected.

Why it is needed: Required on Android 13+ to show any notifications. The monitoring service must display a foreground notification to remain active.
Notifications are generated on-device only. No notification content is sent to any server.
⚙️
Other Permissions (FOREGROUND_SERVICE, RECEIVE_BOOT_COMPLETED, GET_TASKS, REQUEST_DELETE_PACKAGES)
Standard
These are standard Android permissions used to: keep the monitoring service alive as a foreground service, restart monitoring after device reboot (if the user has enabled it), list running tasks on older APIs, and display the system uninstall dialog. None of these involve collecting or transmitting personal data.

4 How Scan Data Is Stored

PrivyScan saves the following data locally on your device only using Android SharedPreferences:

  • Your last privacy score (0–100)
  • A summary of the last 20 scans (app count, risk count, timestamp)
  • App settings such as dark/light theme preference
  • Whether you have completed the onboarding walkthrough

This data is stored in the app's private storage sandbox. It is not accessible to other apps, not backed up to any cloud service (backup is disabled for sensitive scan data), and is permanently deleted when you uninstall PrivyScan.

5 Third-Party Services

PrivyScan does not use any third-party services that collect data, including:

  • No Google Analytics, Firebase, or Crashlytics
  • No advertising SDKs (AdMob, Meta Audience Network, etc.)
  • No crash reporting services (Sentry, Bugsnag, etc.)
  • No cloud sync or backup services

The only network request PrivyScan may make is the PDF export feature, which uses Android's built-in android.graphics.pdf.PdfDocument API — entirely on-device with no network involvement.

6 Data Sharing

PrivyScan does not share any data with any third party, for any purpose, at any time. There is nothing to share — no data leaves your device.

The only way data can leave your device is if you manually choose to export a PDF report and share it yourself using the Android system share sheet. In that case, the data goes to the destination you choose — not to us.

7 Children's Privacy

PrivyScan is not directed at children under 13 years of age and does not knowingly collect any information from children. Since PrivyScan collects no personal data from any user, it poses no specific risk to children's privacy.

8 Changes to This Policy

If this privacy policy is updated, the new version will be published at this URL and the "Last updated" date at the top of this page will be changed. Since PrivyScan does not collect your contact information, we cannot notify you directly — we recommend checking this page if you update the app.

For a FYP project with no backend, significant policy changes are unlikely. Any change that introduces data collection would require explicit user consent.
